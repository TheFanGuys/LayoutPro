Getting GPS…

Stand under a fan and tap Drop. Uses your phone GPS — best near open doors or outdoors; a metal roof weakens it, so watch the ± reading. The faint ring on each fan shows how far off it might be. For tight indoor work, a Google Maps satellite screenshot loaded as a Photo is more accurate.

Column grid (auto)

Count the structural columns each way and drop them as an even grid across the building. It spans the building outline (your drawn walls). Best on a straightened building — rotate it flat first if it came in diagonal. Drag any column after to fine-tune.

Building from size

Type a number — reads feet by default. Flip to miles, or just type the unit: ".236 mi", "1246 ft", "1/4 mile". Outline is drawn to scale (1 ft = 1 unit). Drag the corner dots after to make an L-shape or add-on.

Building from address

Pulls the building outline from OpenStreetMap (free map data — not Google). Works when the building is mapped; many warehouses are. It drops the outline to scale — drag the corner dots to fix anything. Needs internet, and only runs on your live site (not the preview).
